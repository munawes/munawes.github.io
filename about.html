Bio

I've spent my life searching for unique aspects in the mundane and focusing on the why.

My educational focus was in Creative Writing but I've found that the skills of observation and transformation are applicable across many different avenues. Being able to step into the shoes of a fictional character and tell their story is not much different than generating compelling copy for a client whom I've just met. Countless hours spent in the peer-editing process allows me to have a keen eye for syntactical and grammatical composition in a variety of fields from creative and copywriting to technical manuals.

Discovering the why behind things forces me to constantly expand my knowledge base, which is the main reason I embarked on teaching myself Front-End Web Development. Close friends and family members seemed to have trouble accessing the basic functions of web apps (or finding one that fit their needs), so I began to create applications and web pages with accessibility in mind.

Limitations are a thing of the past. Nearly everyone has the ability to find free or low-cost resources for proceeding down their desired path. I aim to be a conduit for that in everything that I do.
